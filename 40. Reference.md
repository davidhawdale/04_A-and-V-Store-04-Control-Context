# Jobs to be Done

## Store Manager JTBD

### Functional Job
*"When I'm running the store, I want to ensure every part of the business—people, tools, and operations—works in sync, so we can hit targets and deliver a consistent, premium experience."*

**Observable behaviours (doing it well):**
- Reviews Shopify dashboard before floor opens daily
- Connects individual tasks to store goals in briefings
- Addresses performance gaps within 48 hours
- Maintains visible metrics board for team

**Warning signs (struggling):**
- Reactive firefighting instead of proactive management
- Team unclear on priorities or how work connects to targets
- Metrics reviewed weekly rather than daily

### Emotional Job
*"I want to feel confident, respected, and in control—knowing I'm leading a high-performing team and delivering an experience I'm proud of."*

**Observable behaviours (doing it well):**
- Delegates significant decisions to trusted associates
- Steps back during client interactions rather than taking over
- Genuine satisfaction when team members succeed
- Composure during pressure moments

### Social Job
*"I want to be seen as a capable, trusted leader—someone who inspires the team, earns recognition from senior leaders, and builds strong customer relationships."*

**Observable behaviours (doing it well):**
- Sought out by other managers for advice
- Head office uses store as best practice example
- Team members reference manager positively to clients

## Store Associate JTBD

### Functional Job
*"When I'm working on the sales floor, I want to deliver seamless, personalised experiences across all channels—so I can delight customers, make sales, and build lasting relationships."*

**Observable behaviours (doing it well):**
- Checks client profile before or during every interaction
- Uses endless aisle to solve stock-outs
- Transaction completion under 3 minutes
- Client leaves with contact for follow-up

**Warning signs (struggling):**
- Treating each visit as new (no profile checks)
- Letting clients leave empty-handed when item available elsewhere
- Queue build-up at checkout

### Emotional Job
*"When I'm with a customer, I want to feel confident about my full use of the app, so I can take pride in providing a great experience."*

**Observable behaviours (doing it well):**
- Navigates Shopify features without hesitation
- Shows clients information on device confidently
- Troubleshoots minor issues independently

### Social Job
*"When I engage with customers, I want to focus on meeting their personalised needs, so I can build real relationships."*

**Observable behaviours (doing it well):**
- Clients request associate by name
- Detailed, actionable notes in client profiles
- Outreach results in appointments or visits
- Clients refer friends to the associate

## Key Principles

2. **Omnichannel seamlessness** — Customers expect unified experience across touchpoints
3. **Data-informed personalisation** — Use purchase history and preferences, not assumptions

# Methods for Critical & Lateral Thinking

## 1. Heuristic Evaluation Audits

**Objective:** Assess workflows against usability heuristics and best practices.

**Process:**
1. Form mixed teams (associates + manager) as audit squads
2. Use Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics as scoring rubric
3. Score each flow 1-5 per heuristic; cite specific breakdowns
4. Prioritise improvement list

## 2. Pre-Mortem & Red Team

**Objective:** Surface hidden risks before feature launches.

**Process:**
1. Convene team before rollout
2. Imagine catastrophic failure six months post-launch
3. Each participant lists potential causes
4. Establish rotating Red Team for ongoing friction identification

## 3. Persona-Driven Role-Play

**Objective:** Empathise with customer journeys and uncover pain points.

**Process:**
1. Use customer personas to script typical journeys
2. Team alternates roles: customer, associate, manager
3. Think-aloud sessions capturing friction
4. Brainstorm lateral fixes

## 4. Competitive Benchmark Audits

**Objective:** Compare workflows against leading solutions.

**Process:**
1. Assign teams to demo parallel workflows on competitor platforms
2. Measure time-on-task, document UI differences
3. Present "stopwatch + screenshot" reports

## 5. Critical Incident Flash Sessions

**Objective:** Rapidly analyse real-world issues as they occur.

**Process:**
1. After any incident, hold 15-minute flash review
2. Answer: What went wrong? Why undetected? How could it have been prevented?
3. Log findings in shared backlog

## 6. Six Thinking Hats

**Objective:** Force multiple perspectives on decisions.

**The Hats:**
- **White:** Facts, data, information
- **Red:** Emotions, intuition, gut feeling
- **Black:** Risks, problems, caution
- **Yellow:** Benefits, optimism, value
- **Green:** Creativity, alternatives, possibilities
- **Blue:** Process, meta-thinking, next steps

## 7. External Mystery Shopping

**Objective:** Validate performance under surprise conditions.

**Process:**
1. Engage mystery shoppers with edge-case requests
2. Collect anonymised support tickets and error logs
3. Debrief and integrate learnings
