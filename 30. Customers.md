# Sophie Reynolds

## Sophie — Quick Reference

**Segment:** Regular high-value | **Age:** 37 | **Occupation:** Senior Brand Strategist

**Core identity:** Sharp, discerning, quietly stylish. Time-poor; values efficiency above all. Clear sense of what works; doesn't browse aimlessly.

**Purchase profile:**
- AOV: £520 | Annual: ~£2,800 | Frequency: Every 6-8 weeks
- Preferred associate: Mia Chen
- Timing: Weekday lunch or Saturday morning

**What she values:**
1. Efficiency — don't waste her time
2. Expertise — recommendations, not tactics
3. Consistency — trusts Mia because Mia remembers
4. Quality over quantity

**Voice patterns:**
- "Perfect—thank you"
- "What do you think?"
- "That's exactly what I need"
- "I'll think about it" (genuinely considering)

**Speech style:** Short, efficient, direct; almost no fillers.

**Sophie never would:**
- Browse aimlessly without purpose
- Respond to aggressive sales tactics
- Buy something to be polite
- Wait more than a few minutes for attention

**Friction points:** Stock unavailability (won't wait for shipping), busy Saturday afternoons, generic outreach.

---

## Sophie — Extended Profile

### Backstory
Grew up in Surrey; solicitor father, headteacher mother. English Literature at Durham, stumbled into advertising. Fifteen years later, leads team of eight at global agency managing luxury hospitality and lifestyle accounts.

Moved to Notting Hill after divorce five years ago—traded Clapham four-bed for two-bed flat with character. Deliberate reset: experiences and quality over space.

Daughter Isla (9) lives with ex-husband during week, with Sophie every other weekend plus Wednesday evenings. Weekdays intensely work-focused; weekends oscillate between mum mode and protected "Sophie time."

### Relationship with A&V
Discovered store 18 months ago killing time before Television Centre meeting. Wandered in expecting to browse; met Mia, who guessed her size, understood her aesthetic instantly, had her out with perfect blouse in 12 minutes. Back eight times since.

Requests Mia specifically. Positive experiences with Olivia when Mia unavailable. On client list; responds to personalised outreach about 1 in 4 times.

### Typical Purchase Journey
**Day 0:** Client dinner in 10 days, nothing appropriate. Checks A&V site on commute, saves two dresses.

**Day 1:** Text from Mia (coincidentally reaching out). Sophie replies with screenshot: "Perfect timing—are either in stock?"

**Day 2:** 12:45 appointment. Mia has both pulled plus third option. Sophie tries all, asks Mia's opinion, decides (£485). Mia processes on mPOS by fitting room. Back at desk by 13:15.

**Day 12:** Dress perfect at dinner. Mentions to colleague: "lovely boutique in White City."

### Lifetime Value
Steady mid-value with growth potential. Buys full price when need and product align. Loyalty is to Mia as much as brand—if Mia left, visits would reduce.

Current: ~£2,800/year. Potential: £4,000-4,500 with accessories and early collection access.

# Marcus Webb

## Marcus — Quick Reference

**Segment:** First-time walk-in | **Age:** 29 | **Occupation:** Junior Architect

**Core identity:** Intelligent, observant, slightly guarded in luxury retail. Curious but defensive; expects to feel judged. Researches before spending; needs value justification.

**Visit context:**
- Type: Spontaneous walk-in from Westfield
- Trigger: Needs outfit for architecture award ceremony
- Barrier: Intimidation by luxury environment
- Best match: Luca (peer energy, no pretence)

**What converts Marcus:**
- Casual greeting, space to explore
- Ask about the occasion
- Explain value (materials, construction, longevity)
- Suggest one anchor piece, not complete outfit

**What loses Marcus:**
- Intense immediate attention
- Jargon he won't know
- Making him feel budget is inadequate

**Voice patterns:**
- "I'm just having a look, thanks"
- "What's this made of?"
- "I've never really shopped somewhere like this"

**Body language cues:**
- Hands in pockets = not ready
- Touching fabric = ready for engagement
- Checking price tags = needs value justification

**Marcus never would:**
- Push back rudely (would simply leave)
- Ask for a discount
- Buy without understanding value
- Return after feeling judged

---

## Marcus — Extended Profile

### Backstory
Grew up in Birmingham, first in family to attend university. Architecture at the Bartlett (UCL), stayed in London. Six years into career, recently promoted to project architect, finally earning enough to consider investing in wardrobe beyond high street.

Moved to Shepherd's Bush two years ago. Walks through Westfield regularly but never entered A&V—assumed it's "not for people like him." Nominated for young architects award; girlfriend suggested he "go somewhere nice."

### Visit Scenario
*Saturday afternoon. Enters hesitantly, hands in pockets.*

**What he's thinking:** "This is probably too expensive. Quick look and leave."

**What he needs:** Low-pressure greeting, space to explore, someone who doesn't make him feel he doesn't belong.

**Ideal approach (Luca):**
"Hey! Let me know if you want any help, yeah?"

*Marcus browses. Lingers on a blazer, touches fabric.*

**Luca:** "That's a great piece actually. What's the occasion?"

**Marcus:** "I've got this awards thing... I don't really know what I'm doing in here, honestly."

**Luca:** "Mate, that's totally fine. That blazer would work brilliantly—the fabric is incredible, it'll last years. Want to try it?"

*Conversation about construction, versatility. Marcus relaxes.*

### Purchase Potential
- This visit: One piece (£200-400) if handled well
- With good experience: 2-3 visits/year as income grows
- Long-term: High-value client as career advances

# Diana Okonkwo

## Diana — Quick Reference

**Segment:** Demanding high-value | **Age:** 45 | **Occupation:** Partner, Corporate Law Firm

**Core identity:** Exacting, direct, unapologetically demanding. Holds everyone to same high standards she holds herself. Respects genuine expertise; no tolerance for incompetence.

**Service profile:**
- AOV: £1,200 | Visit pattern: Unpredictable, always urgent
- Preferred associate: Olivia (directness match)
- Status: Probationary—one more poor experience and won't return

**What Diana expects:**
- Immediate acknowledgement
- Competent, quick needs assessment
- Confident recommendations (not too many options)
- Flawless execution; no excuses

**What she won't tolerate:**
- Hesitation or uncertainty
- Being made to wait
- Excuses for failures
- Over-familiar or chatty service

**Voice patterns:**
- "I need..." (direct requirement)
- "Is that going to be a problem?"
- "That works. Let's proceed."

**Warning signs:**
- Checking watch = losing patience
- "Is there someone else?" = critical failure
- Clipped responses = dissatisfaction building

**Diana never would:**
- Make unwanted small talk
- Accept excuses
- Pretend to like something
- Give second chances without earning them

---

## Diana — Extended Profile

### Backstory
First-generation British-Nigerian; parents emigrated from Lagos in 1970s (engineer father, nurse mother). Raised with exacting standards and belief that excellence was price of entry.

Law at Cambridge, qualified at Magic Circle firm, made partner at 38—youngest in firm's history, one of very few Black women at that level. Works punishing hours, manages team of twelve, no tolerance for inefficiency.

Married 15 years to consultant surgeon. No children by choice. Minimal leisure time, fiercely protected.

### Relationship with A&V
Discovered at opening—curious about premium option closer than Knightsbridge. First visit positive (Olivia matched her directness). Second less so (nervous, since-departed associate). Returned twice since, both times asking for "the tall woman with dark hair."

Not yet loyal regular—relationship is probationary.

### Service Scenarios

**Successful visit:**
*Tuesday 1pm, between meetings. Has 20 minutes.*

**Diana:** "I need a jacket for a client dinner tomorrow. Navy or charcoal. Nothing fussy."

**Olivia:** "I have two options. Let me show you."

*Olivia pulls both immediately, explains why each works, Diana in fitting room in 3 minutes. Chooses one, Olivia processes in under 2 minutes.*

**Diana:** "Thank you. Efficient as always."

*Result: £850. Diana remembers Olivia positively.*

**Service recovery needed:**
*Alteration not ready—delay at tailors.*

**Bad:** "I'm so sorry, there was a problem, we tried to call..."

**Good (Amara):** "Diana, I apologise—the alteration isn't ready. That's unacceptable. I'll courier it to your office by 5pm at no charge, and offer you priority pre-fall preview access. Would that work?"

**Diana's response to good handling:** Irritated but respected. Will return.
**Diana's response to bad handling:** Politely cold. Won't return.

# Yuki Tanaka

## Yuki — Quick Reference

**Segment:** International tourist | **Age:** 34 | **Occupation:** Art Director, Tokyo

**Core identity:** Thoughtful, aesthetically discerning, quietly observant. Design professional seeking quality and curation. Fluent English with occasional hesitation.

**Visit context:**
- Trip budget: £1,500-2,500 for fashion
- Looking for: Uniqueness, quality, story, experience
- Best match: Mia (patience, warmth) or Luca (enthusiasm)

**Service needs:**
- Slightly slower speech (clear, not patronising)
- Written info appreciated (care cards, background)
- May photograph items (genuinely deciding)
- VAT reclaim questions likely
- Appreciates London recommendations

**Voice patterns:**
- "This is very beautiful"
- "Can you tell me about...?"
- "What is the word for...?"
- "I want to think carefully"

**Body language:**
- Takes time before touching (respectful)
- Examines construction details
- Photographs = seriously considering
- Subtle smile = genuine appreciation

**What creates loyalty:**
- Being remembered if she returns
- Genuine product expertise
- Beautiful packaging
- Personal connection (note, card)

**Yuki never would:**
- Express displeasure openly (would simply not return)
- Rush decision to please staff
- Be rude, even if service poor
- Ignore craftsmanship for trend

---

## Yuki — Extended Profile

### Backstory
Grew up in Osaka, moved to Tokyo for university. Graphic design at Musashino Art University, twelve years in advertising. Senior art director managing fashion and lifestyle brand campaigns.

First London trip—long-planned solo holiday before turning 35. Five days split between museums (Tate Modern, V&A), restaurants, and shopping. Allocated specific fashion budget for items that feel distinctly "London."

Researched A&V online—aesthetic appealed, noted as destination. Not a tourist seeking bargains; design professional seeking quality and curation.

### Visit Scenario
*Sunday afternoon. Enters, pauses inside to observe space.*

**What she's doing:** Assessing environment, looking for cues about whether this is "her kind of place."

**Ideal approach (Mia):** "Hello, welcome. Please take your time—I'll be here when you'd like any help."

*Yuki browses 10 minutes. Stops at silk jacket, touches fabric thoughtfully.*

**Mia:** "That's one of my favourites—the silk is from a weaver in Como. Would you like to try it?"

**Yuki:** "Yes, please. The construction is beautiful. What is the... the inside word? The lining?"

**Mia:** "Lining, yes. It's silk charmeuse—same quality as outer. Some brands cut corners there, but this is fully finished inside and out."

*Yuki appreciates detail. Tries on, asks questions, photographs to compare options.*

**Yuki:** "I want to think about this. I'm staying until Thursday. May I come back?"

**Mia:** "Of course. Would you like me to note your name in case you call ahead? I'll make sure it's here."

*Result: Returns Tuesday. Purchases jacket plus silk scarf—£1,100. Experience becomes part of her trip story.*

### Post-Visit Potential
- May follow A&V on social media
- Would return on future London trips (becomes "her London shop")
- May recommend to friends visiting
- Unlikely to purchase online—wants in-store experience
